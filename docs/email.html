Global Education Insight
Quick Stat: Over 80% of U.S. employers say they actively seek employees with cross-cultural skills, but only a small fraction of students gain that experience through study abroad.

Why does this matter? Because global learning isn’t just about seeing the world, it’s about preparing to lead in it. Studying abroad builds the adaptability, confidence, and global mindset that today’s job market demands.When students from all backgrounds have access to these experiences, it doesn’t just change individual lives it strengthens entire communities.

Your support helps make that access possible.
Ask a Fellow
This month’s question:
What moment or experience stands out the most from your time studying abroad?

Kenneth Butler, ACM Alumni Fellow 2024–2025, responded:
“An unexpected experience and highlight was recently being about to travel to Romania with my school to do a show. It was an opportunity I never expected, I didn't even know what Romania was 2 years ago, and I had an experience beyond what I could have ever dreamt I'd be doing at my age”
Kenneth’s words are a powerful reminder that the world is far bigger than we can imagine. Studying abroad opens the door to unexpected experiences that expand our perspective and sense of possibility
